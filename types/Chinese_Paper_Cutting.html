Introduction

Chinese paper cutting art, or Jianzhi (剪纸), is a Chinese traditional paper cutting style that evolved from cutting patterns for expensive Chinese embroidery to become a folk art. Jianzhi can be dated back to at least the 6th century AD. It is used for a variety of purposes in Chinese culture, almost all of which are related to health, prosperity, or decoration. As a result, the color red is the most common in Chinese culture to be used in paper cutting art. This type of paper cutting art is usually done solely with scissors; however, as time moved on, people also began to use tools such as the graver to assist.
